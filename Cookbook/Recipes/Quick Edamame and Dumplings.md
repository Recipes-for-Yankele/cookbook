# Quick Edamame & Dumplings

> I want Asian food, I want it now, and I don't know what to do with the rice in the fridge. Reasonable protein and veg, low cost. Quick but there is some timing math involved.

---

## Ingredients

- 1 cup frozen edamame
- 5–10 frozen dumplings *(depending on brand and hunger)*
- 1 heaping tbsp gochujang
- 1 heaping tbsp miso paste
- 1 tbsp chili oil
- Sesame oil
- Green onions, chopped
- Asian-style noodles *(optional — avoid fried noodles like instant ramen)*
- Cooked white rice *(for serving, if not doing noodles)*

---

## Method

1. Bring a pot of water to a rolling boil.
2. Add frozen dumplings first — they take the longest. Boil per package instructions.
3. **If doing the noodle variant:** Add noodles with enough lead time to finish cooking without overcooking the dumplings. Do the math based on package times.
4. With 2–3 minutes left on the dumplings, add frozen edamame.
5. Once edamame is cooked, drain and keep everything in the pot.
6. Over low heat, add gochujang, miso paste, green onions, chili oil, and sesame oil. Stir frequently so nothing sticks.
   - Add a splash of water if the sauce isn't coming together or dumplings are sticking.
   - Ideally dissolve miso in a little water first, but you can mix it straight in.
7. Serve over white rice. If doing noodles, skip the rice.

---

## Notes

- A reliable pantry staple — always worth keeping frozen edamame stocked for this.
- No soy sauce needed; miso + gochujang + chili oil provide plenty of salt.
