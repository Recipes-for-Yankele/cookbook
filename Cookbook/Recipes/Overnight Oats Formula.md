# Overnight Oats Formula

> Oh no, it's 10pm and I need lunch for tomorrow. Filling, high-protein oatmeal sludge with customizable flavor profiles so it never gets boring.

---

## Base Ingredients

- ½ cup rolled oats
- 2–3 spoonfuls non-fat Greek yogurt *(essential — do not skip)*
- ¼–½ cup milk *(or water in a pinch, but milk is better)*
- Sweetener: honey, maple syrup, or jam
- 1 scoop protein powder (vanilla or chocolate)
- A flavorful spread as the main flavor driver: jam, orange marmalade, Nutella, or peanut butter

---

## Flavor Profiles

### Chocolate
- Chocolate protein powder
- Chocolate chips
- Chopped dates
- Blueberries or other fruit
- Peanut butter or Nutella swirled in
- 1 tsp hot cocoa mix
- Tiny bit of instant coffee for depth *(optional)*

### Vanilla / Fruity
- Vanilla protein powder
- Frozen raspberries or blueberries *(frozen is cheaper than fresh)*
- Honey or frozen coconut
- Orange marmalade for a tart option
- Trader Joe's lemon curd + blueberries is excellent

### Cinnamon Apple
- Vanilla protein powder
- Chopped apple
- Cinnamon
- Maple syrup
- Peanut butter
- TJ's honeycrisp apple granola on top *(seasonal bonus)*

---

## Method

1. Combine oats, yogurt, milk, and protein powder in a container and mix until homogeneous.
2. Add toppings and swirls however you like on top — no need to mix them in.
3. Adjust milk quantity: it should be slightly runny when assembled; the oats will firm up overnight.
4. Refrigerate overnight. Serve cold.
5. Keep granola separate until serving to preserve crunch.
6. If using a sugary jam or Nutella, keep it in one corner and swirl through as you eat rather than mixing it fully.

---

## Notes

- Getting the texture right is a feel you develop over time. Eventually you stop measuring.
- You need fat — peanut butter, full-fat yogurt, or both — otherwise it won't be filling.
- A full scoop of protein powder brings this to ~40g protein with the yogurt and milk combined. Use half a scoop if that's too heavy.
- Sliced strawberries don't hold up overnight. Use them whole or serve on the side.
- Dried mango or other dried fruit rehydrates nicely in the oats.
- Cocoa powder + sweetener can substitute for hot cocoa mix, but the mix is easier.
