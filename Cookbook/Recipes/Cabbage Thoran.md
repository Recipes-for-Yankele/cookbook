# Cabbage Thoran

> A quick South Indian side dish. Cabbage is cheap, lasts forever, and this comes together fast.

---

## Ingredients

- ½ head cabbage, thinly sliced
- 1 small red onion, chopped
- Mustard seeds
- Cumin seeds
- Turmeric
- Cumin powder
- Coriander powder *(if available)*
- Kitchen King masala *(optional, works well here)*
- Ginger paste
- ¼–⅓ cup frozen grated coconut, thawed
- 1 small hot pepper (e.g. Thai chili), chopped
- Curry leaves or bay leaves
- Salt
- Oil *(coconut oil for authenticity; canola or olive oil works fine)*

---

## Method

1. Slice cabbage and onion on a large cutting board — this takes up space.
2. Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high.
3. Add mustard seeds. Once they start to pop, add cumin seeds. → [[Techniques/Tempering Seeds in Oil|Tempering Seeds in Oil]]
4. Before the seeds burn, add onion.
5. Add ginger paste. Cook until onions are turning golden. → [[Techniques/Garlic and Ginger Paste Base|Garlic & Ginger Paste Base]]
6. Add curry leaves (or bay leaves) and chopped chili. Fry briefly.
7. Add turmeric, cumin, salt, and any other spices. → [[Techniques/Blooming Spices|Blooming Spices]]
8. Add cabbage last. Cook on medium heat, stirring, until it loses about half its volume — roughly 5 minutes. Do not overcook.
9. In the last minute, stir in the thawed frozen coconut.

---

## Notes

- Don't overcook the cabbage — beyond 5 minutes and it becomes gassy. Medium heat, done when volume is halved.
- Makes 4 servings for about $3.
- Coconut is non-negotiable for the authentic South Indian character.
