# Cooking a Garlic & Ginger Paste Base

Frying garlic and ginger paste together in oil at the start of a dish builds the aromatic foundation for most Asian and South Asian recipes. Getting this step right makes a meaningful difference.

## How to Do It

1. After onions have softened and begun to brown, push them aside and add garlic (and ginger if using) to the center of the pan where it's hottest.
2. Fry, stirring, until the **raw smell is gone** — this usually takes 1–2 minutes on medium heat.
3. The paste will darken slightly and stick a little to the pan. That's good.
4. Add your next ingredient (spices, tomato, sauce) before it burns.

## How to Know It's Done

- The sharp, pungent raw garlic smell gives way to a sweeter, more mellow aroma.
- The paste tightens and dries slightly against the pan.

## Watch Out For

- **Burnt garlic is bitter and ruins the dish.** Keep the heat moderate and don't walk away.
- Garlic paste burns faster than chopped garlic because it has more surface area exposed to the pan.
