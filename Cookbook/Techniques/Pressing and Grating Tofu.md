# Pressing & Grating Tofu

Removing moisture from tofu before cooking is the single biggest factor in getting it crispy. The less water inside, the more it browns instead of steams.

## Methods

### Box Grater (Best for Crispy Baking)
Grate firm or extra-firm tofu on the large holes of a box grater directly onto the baking sheet. This maximizes surface area and produces the crispiest result when baked.

### Hand-Crumble + Squeeze
Break the tofu into rough crumbles with your hands, then squeeze each handful firmly over the sink to wring out water. Quicker than grating, slightly less even texture.

### Press with Weight *(for slices/cubes)*
Wrap tofu in a clean towel or paper towels, place on a flat surface, and set a heavy pan on top for 15–30 minutes. Good when you want defined shapes.

## Tips

- **Extra-firm tofu** has less water to begin with — always the better choice for baking or frying.
- After grating or crumbling, toss with marinade (soy sauce, mirin, chili oil) and let it sit briefly before baking.
- The tofu will shrink significantly during baking as remaining moisture evaporates. Don't be surprised by how little you end up with.
