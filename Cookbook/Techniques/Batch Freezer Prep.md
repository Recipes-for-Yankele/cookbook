# Batch Freezer Prep

Making components in bulk and freezing them in single-serving portions so you can have restaurant-quality flavor bases on demand with zero effort on weeknights.

## The Idea

Some flavor bases — miso, aromatics, curry pastes — take time to make but keep perfectly in the freezer for months. Making a batch once and portioning into individual servings means the hard work is already done.

## How to Portion

- **Balls / portions:** Roll into golf-ball-sized or smaller units (see [[Techniques/Miso Soup Freezer Balls|Miso Soup Freezer Balls]] approach). Smaller than you think — these are concentrated.
- **Ice cube tray:** Great for sauces and pastes. Pop out cubes and store in a bag once frozen.
- **Flat bag method:** Spread thin in a zip-lock bag, freeze flat, then snap off portions as needed.

## Storage Tips

- Label with the date and contents.
- Most flavor pastes and bases keep 2–3 months in the freezer before quality degrades.
- Freeze flat when possible — faster to freeze, faster to thaw.
