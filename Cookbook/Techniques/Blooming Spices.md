# Blooming Spices

Adding ground spices directly to hot oil or fat before adding liquid, so they toast and release their full aroma rather than just dissolving into the dish.

## How to Do It

1. After cooking your aromatics (onion, garlic, ginger), push them to the side or keep them in the Pan.
2. Add your ground spices directly to the hot oil. The residual heat is usually enough — you don't need screaming-hot oil.
3. Stir constantly for **20–30 seconds**. You're looking for the spices to darken slightly and become very fragrant.
4. Add liquid (tomato purée, stock, water) **immediately** to stop the spices from burning.

## Why It Works

Heat activates fat-soluble flavor compounds in spices that water alone can't extract. The difference between toasted and untoasted spices in the same dish is significant.

## Watch Out For

- **Burning:** Ground spices go from bloomed to burnt fast. Have your liquid ready before you add the spices.
- **Too much oil:** Spices need direct contact with fat, not a puddle of it.
