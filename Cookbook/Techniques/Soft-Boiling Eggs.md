# Soft-Boiling Eggs

Cooking eggs so the white is fully set but the yolk is jammy and barely set in the center. The standard for ramen eggs, Mayak eggs, and most marinated egg preparations.

## How to Do It

1. Bring a pot of water to a full boil.
2. Add **1 tsp white vinegar** — this helps the whites set faster if a shell cracks.
3. Gently lower eggs in with a spoon (room temperature eggs are less likely to crack).
4. Cook for exactly **6 minutes** for a jammy yolk.
5. Immediately transfer to an **ice bath** (bowl of cold water + ice) for at least 5 minutes to stop cooking.
6. Peel once fully cooled.

## Timing Reference

| Time | Yolk Result |
|------|-------------|
| 6 min | Jammy, barely set center |
| 7 min | Mostly set, slightly soft |
| 8 min | Fully set, still moist |
| 10–12 min | Hard-boiled |

## Why the Ice Bath Matters

Eggs keep cooking from residual heat after leaving the water. Skipping the ice bath will overcook the yolk even if your timing was perfect.
