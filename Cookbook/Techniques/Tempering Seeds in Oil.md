# Tempering Seeds in Oil

A South and Southeast Asian technique of frying whole spice seeds in oil at the start of cooking so they pop, crackle, and infuse the oil with flavor before anything else goes in.

## How to Do It

1. Heat oil in the pan until it shimmers — hot enough that a seed dropped in sizzles immediately.
2. Add **mustard seeds** first. They take longer to pop.
3. Once mustard seeds begin to pop and dance, add **cumin seeds**.
4. As soon as the seeds are toasted and fragrant (10–15 seconds), add your onion or other aromatics to stop the seeds from burning.

## Why It Works

Whole seeds contain volatile oils that only release when exposed to high heat. Blooming them in oil before anything else builds a flavor base that permeates the entire dish.

## Watch Out For

- **Don't walk away.** Seeds go from perfect to burnt in seconds.
- **The lid trick:** Mustard seeds pop and fly. You can loosely cover the pan right after adding them.
